WeatherBird Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026

WeatherBird is a free Android weather app. This policy explains what information the app uses and how it is handled.

Information We Collect

WeatherBird does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. The app does not require an account, login, or registration of any kind.

The only data used by the app is:

  • ZIP codes you enter manually. These are stored locally on your device so your saved locations persist between sessions. They are never sent to us or any third party directly.
  • Derived coordinates (latitude/longitude). When you add a location, your ZIP code is converted to geographic coordinates so weather data can be fetched. This lookup is sent to the Nominatim geocoding service (operated by OpenStreetMap Foundation).

Third-Party Services

WeatherBird fetches weather data from third-party APIs. Your approximate location (latitude and longitude) is sent to these services as part of normal app operation:

  • Open-Meteo — weather forecasts and air quality data (open-meteo.com/en/terms)
  • Nominatim / OpenStreetMap — ZIP code to coordinate lookup (osmfoundation.org/wiki/Privacy_Policy)
  • National Weather Service (NWS) — severe weather alerts (weather.gov/privacy)

These services receive only the coordinates needed to return weather data. WeatherBird has no control over and no access to data those services may retain. Please review their individual privacy policies for details.

Data Storage

Your saved locations and cached weather data are stored locally on your device only. Nothing is uploaded to any server operated by WeatherBird. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data.

Advertising and Analytics

WeatherBird contains no advertisements and no analytics or tracking SDKs. The Android Advertising ID is not accessed or used.

Children

WeatherBird is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance.
